Exposure Defined

Simply put, Exposure is the condition of being exposed. In terms of social media, this occurs from the accumulation of small pieces of information over multiple outlets that can be connected to provide more information than the user intended. Hackers, prospective employers, criminals and child predators can acquire a disturbing amount of information about a person from these small pieces of information, which have been freely distributed on social media outlets. SXProXimity classifies exposure based on what is being exposed about the user: Who they are, What they are doing and Where they are located.

Managing Exposure is Difficult

Social media is now ubiquitous; everyone from pre-teens to grandparents are using facebook, twitter, tumblr and many others to keep in touch with friends and family, promote their products and gather information. These posts have two attributes that make them particularly difficult to manage: a global audience can observe them and they are effectively permanent. Once a post catches the interest of an unwanted observer, this observer can find other posts and start making connections.

It is practically impossible for a person to keep track of everything that they have posted over several years and multiple outlets without a tool built for this purpose. Privacy settings are inadequate for this purpose.
